Neutron Star

A neutron star is the collapsed core of a massive supergiant star that has undergone a supernova explosion. Composed almost entirely of neutrons, they are incredibly denseâ€”so dense that a single teaspoon of neutron star material would weigh around a billion tons on Earth.

These stars are typically only 20 kilometers in diameter but can have masses up to twice that of our Sun. Neutron stars often rotate at incredibly high speeds and emit beams of radiation, making them observable as pulsars when the beam sweeps past Earth.

Neutron stars are key objects in astrophysics for studying matter under extreme conditions, gravitational waves, and the fundamental laws of physics.
