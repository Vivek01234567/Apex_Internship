Supergiant Star

Supergiant stars are among the largest and most luminous stars in the universe. These colossal stars have radii hundreds to thousands of times that of our Sun and can shine millions of times brighter.

They represent the late stages of evolution for massive stars and are found in both red and blue forms, depending on temperature and age. Red supergiants like Betelgeuse are cooler and more expanded, while blue supergiants are hotter and younger.

Supergiants often end their lives in spectacular supernova explosions, leaving behind neutron stars or black holes.
