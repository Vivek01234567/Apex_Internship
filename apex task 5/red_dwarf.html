Red Dwarf

Red dwarfs are the most common type of star in the Milky Way galaxy. These small and relatively cool stars burn their fuel very slowly, which gives them lifespans ranging from tens of billions to even trillions of years. They are often not visible to the naked eye from Earth due to their low brightness, but they are abundant in number.

Because of their long lives, red dwarfs are key to studying stellar evolution. Some notable red dwarfs include Proxima Centauri, the closest known star to the Sun. Planets orbiting red dwarfs are also considered in the search for extraterrestrial life.
